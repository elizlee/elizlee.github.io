About

Thanks for stopping by!

I am a research engineer at the USC Information Sciences Institute. Since I started in July 2019, I have written programs to monitor and process annotation data, assisted in designing annotation tasks, and contributed my skills towards the development of web tools and semantic extraction systems. Prior to that, I had completed my M.S. program in Computational Linguistics at the University of Rochester. During my time there, I took courses in linguistics and computer science while assisting with research for the Quantitative Semantics Lab and the TRIPS Lab.

Broadly my interests are in natural language processing, software engineering, artificial intelligence, UX design, semantics, and second-language acquisition. I also hope to gain more experience in developing web applications and become proficient in Japanese.

Aside from all things computational and linguistic, my interests include fiction writing, video games, and bubble tea.
